Chats vs Projects: 5 Key Differences

1. Persistence & Storage — Chats are ephemeral; projects have a persistent workspace folder that survives between sessions, allowing you to build on prior work.

2. Context Continuity — Chats start fresh each time; projects maintain full context across sessions via files, memory, and artifact history, so Claude remembers what you were building.

3. File Organization — Chats have no dedicated storage; projects give you a structured folder to organize deliverables, data, and artifacts in one place.

4. Artifacts — Both support artifacts, but in projects, artifacts persist and you can reference them across sessions; in chats, they're transactional.

5. Collaboration & Handoff — Projects are designed for ongoing work and team handoffs; chats are better for quick questions or one-off tasks.
